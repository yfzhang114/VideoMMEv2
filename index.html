Introduction

介绍

In recent years, Video Multimodal Large Language Models have made significant progress on various perception and reasoning tasks, marking a shift from simple video recognition to deeper semantic understanding and complex reasoning. Despite these breakthroughs, existing evaluation methods still focus primarily on narrow and task-specific benchmarks, making it difficult to determine whether models truly understand video content or merely exploit dataset biases and spurious correlations.

Meanwhile, in complex video reasoning tasks, previous research has focused on evaluating specific reasoning abilities while less attention has been paid to models' overall reasoning coherence in temporal and causal relation problems. This leaves us uncertain whether models are conducting systematic reasoning or merely generating locally consistent answers. These limitations hinder comprehensive evaluation of state-of-the-art Video MLLMs and highlight the urgent need to deeply investigate models' true understanding and reliable reasoning capabilities in video comprehension.

💡 Our Solution

To address these challenges, we propose Video-MME v2, a comprehensive benchmark designed to systematically evaluate the true understanding and reasoning capabilities of Video MLLMs. This benchmark introduces a group-based evaluation approach, systematically analyzing state-of-the-art models from two complementary dimensions.

🎯

Capability Consistency

Examines the stability and breadth of specific fundamental perception capabilities across different task dimensions (e.g., from viewpoint transformation to spatial reconstruction for spatial understanding) and granularities (e.g., from object counting to motion counting for video counting).

🔗

Reasoning Coherence

Evaluates models' multi-step reasoning capabilities through a series of temporally and causally related questions, detecting whether models can make complex high-level inferences along logical chains.

Overall, these two dimensions together form a rigorous and interpretable evaluation benchmark that focuses not only on task performance but also measures models' systematic and reliable reasoning capabilities in complex video scenarios.

近年来，视频多模态大语言模型在多种感知与推理任务上取得了显著进展，标志着该领域正从单纯的视频识别迈向更深层次的语义理解与复杂推理。尽管取得了较大突破，现有评测方法仍主要关注于狭窄且任务特定的基准，难以判断模型是否真正理解视频内容，或仅仅利用了数据集中的偏好与假性相关。

与此同时，在复杂的视频推理任务中，以往研究多聚焦于某一特定推理能力的评估，而较少考察模型在时间与因果关联问题中的整体推理连贯性，这使得我们尚不清楚模型是在进行系统性推理，还是仅生成局部一致的答案。这些局限阻碍了对前沿视频 MLLMs 的全面评估，也凸显出迫切需要深入探究模型在视频理解中真实理解与可靠推理能力的必要性。

💡 我们的解决方案

为应对上述挑战，我们提出了 Video-MME v2，这是一个旨在系统评估视频 MLLMs 真实理解与推理能力的综合性基准。该基准通过引入基于任务分组（group-based）的评测方式，从两个互补维度对前沿模型进行系统分析。

🎯

能力一致性 (Capability Consistency)

用于检验特定基础感知能力在不同任务维度（如从视角转换到空间重建体现空间理解）与粒度（如从目标计数到运动计数体现视频计数）变化下的稳定性与广度。

🔗

推理连贯性 (Reasoning Coherence)

用于评估模型的多步推理能力，通过一系列时间与因果相关的问题检测模型是否能够沿逻辑链条作出复杂的高层次推断。

总体而言，这两个维度共同构成了一个严谨且可解释的评测基准，不仅关注模型的任务表现，更衡量其在复杂视频场景中系统性与可靠性的推理能力。

To comprehensively evaluate the video understanding capabilities of multimodal large models, Video-MME V2 has constructed a progressive hierarchical classification system. We have abandoned flat task stacking and instead divided capability dimensions into three cognitive stages: from basic information retrieval and aggregation, advancing to dynamic capture of temporal sequences, actions, and changes, and ultimately rising to complex reasoning about plots, the physical world, and social behaviors.

📊 Three-Level Capability Hierarchy

LEVEL 1

Retrieval & Aggregation

Basic visual information recognition and extraction capabilities, including object recognition, attribute judgment, scene understanding, and other perception-level tasks.

LEVEL 2

Temporal & Dynamic Understanding

Dynamic capture of temporal sequences, actions, and changes, including action recognition, state change tracking, temporal order understanding, and other cognitive-level tasks.

LEVEL 3

Complex Reasoning

Complex reasoning about plots, the physical world, and social behaviors, including causal reasoning, intention understanding, common sense inference, and other high-level reasoning tasks.

🔧 Annotation Pipeline

✓ Fully Human Expert-Led
1
Video Source Selection

Select 3-30 minute medium-length videos and the latest content from 2025 onwards to ensure data richness and timeliness

2
Q&A Design

Manually construct strong distractors, forcing models to answer based on video content rather than background knowledge

3
Expert Cross-Review

Multiple rounds of manual verification and correction, eliminating ambiguous expressions and non-unique answers

This purely manual pipeline maximally eliminates noise and hallucinations from machine-generated data, ensuring high quality and reliability of the evaluation data.

为了全面评估多模态大模型的视频理解能力，Video-MME V2 构建了一套循序渐进的层级化分类体系。我们摒弃了扁平化的任务堆叠，而是将能力维度划分为三个认知阶段：从基础的信息检索与聚合，进阶到对时序、动作与变化的动态捕捉，最终上升至对剧情、物理世界及社会行为的复杂推理。

📊 三级能力层级体系

LEVEL 1

信息检索与聚合 (Retrieval & Aggregation)

基础的视觉信息识别与提取能力，包括对象识别、属性判断、场景理解等感知层任务。

LEVEL 2

时序与动态理解 (Temporal & Dynamic)

对时序、动作与变化的动态捕捉，包括动作识别、状态变化跟踪、时间顺序理解等认知层任务。

LEVEL 3

复杂推理 (Complex Reasoning)

对剧情、物理世界及社会行为的复杂推理，包括因果推理、意图理解、常识推断等高阶推理任务。

🔧 标注流程

✓ 完全由人类专家主导
1
视频源筛选

选取3-30分钟的中长视频及2025年后的最新素材，保证数据的丰富度与时效性

2
问答设计

人工构建强干扰项，迫使模型必须基于视频内容而非背景知识作答

3
专家交叉评审

多轮人工校验修正，剔除模糊表述及答案不唯一的情况

这一纯人工流水线最大程度地消除了机器生成数据的噪声与幻觉，确保评测数据的高质量与可靠性。

🔍 Case Study: Multi-step Reasoning案例研究：多步推理

Case Comparison
QUESTION (Level 3 - Causal Reasoning)
In the video, after the red ball hits the blue ball, why does the blue ball move to the right instead of to the left? Please analyze based on the physical scene shown in the video.
视频中，当红色小球撞击蓝色小球后，蓝色小球为什么会向右移动而不是向左？请根据视频中展示的物理场景进行分析。
GPT-5.2-high
✓ Correct
Based on the collision scene shown in the video, the red ball hits the stationary blue ball at high speed from the left. According to the law of conservation of momentum, momentum is transferred along the collision direction after impact, so the blue ball moves to the right...
根据视频中展示的碰撞场景，红色小球从左侧以较高速度撞击静止的蓝色小球。根据动量守恒定律，碰撞后动量会沿着碰撞方向传递，因此蓝色小球会向右侧移动...
Gemini-3-Pro
✓ Correct
The video shows the red ball rolling from the left side of the screen and hitting the blue ball. Since the red ball's direction of motion is from left to right, when the two balls collide, the red ball transfers its momentum to the blue ball, causing the blue ball to move in the same direction (to the right).
视频显示红球从画面左侧滚动并撞击蓝球。由于红球的运动方向是从左向右，当两球发生碰撞时，红球将其动量传递给蓝球，导致蓝球沿相同方向（向右）运动。
Model-X-7B
✗ Incorrect
The blue ball moves to the right because the table is tilted, and the ball naturally rolls toward the lower side. The red ball's collision only triggered this movement.
蓝色小球向右移动是因为桌面是倾斜的，球会自然滚向低处。红色小球的碰撞只是触发了这一运动。
💡 Key Insight关键洞察

This case demonstrates the differences between models in physical reasoning tasks. High-performance models can correctly identify collision dynamics, while weaker models tend to produce "shortcut reasoning," attributing motion to non-existent scene factors.

此案例展示了模型在物理推理任务中的差异。高性能模型能够正确识别碰撞动力学，而较弱模型容易产生"捷径推理"，将运动归因于不存在的场景因素。

🕐 Case Study: Temporal Understanding案例研究：时序理解

Case
QUESTION (Level 2 - Temporal Ordering)
Please arrange the following events in chronological order as they appear in the video: A) Man opens refrigerator B) Woman enters kitchen C) Dog runs across living room D) Phone rings
请按照视频中事件发生的先后顺序排列：A) 男子打开冰箱 B) 女子进入厨房 C) 狗跑过客厅 D) 电话铃响
Qwen3-VL-235B
✓ Correct
The correct order is: D → C → B → A. First the phone rings (0:05), then the dog is startled by the sound and runs across the living room (0:08), the woman hears the sound and walks from the bedroom to the kitchen (0:15), and finally the man opens the refrigerator in the kitchen (0:23).
正确顺序是：D → C → B → A。首先电话铃响（0:05），随后狗被声音惊动跑过客厅（0:08），女子听到声音后从卧室走向厨房（0:15），最后男子在厨房打开冰箱（0:23）。
Model-Y-13B
✗ Incorrect
The order should be: B → A → C → D. The woman first appears in the kitchen, then the man opens the refrigerator...
顺序应该是：B → A → C → D。女子首先出现在厨房，然后男子打开冰箱...
💡 Key Insight关键洞察

Temporal understanding is one of the core capabilities of video comprehension. This case shows that many models make errors when handling multi-event temporal ordering, especially when events are closely spaced or causally related.

时序理解是视频理解的核心能力之一。此案例表明，许多模型在处理多事件时序排列时容易出错，尤其是当事件间隔较短或存在因果关联时。
